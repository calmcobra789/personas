Personas in Design

The Serengeti, Tanzania, 2016

Personas and Storyboarding

Part 1: Preparation

Chosen accessible interface: Westinghouse TV Remote.

Questions to ask users:

  • What do you typically do or watch on the TV when using this remote?
  • How often do you watch TV?
  • Do you know what all of the remote’s buttons do?
  • What percentage of the remote’s 46 buttons do you use?
  • Could you name your 5 most used buttons on the remote?
  • Do you keep the lights on or off when you watch TV?

    • If respondent indicates that they always watch TV with the lights on, move on to the next question.

    • Else, tell respondent: In the dark, just by feeling the buttons, I want you to perform tasks using your 5 most used buttons to judge how accurately you can do so.

    Part 2: Recording Observations

    Summary of answers:

  • Most people use a Streaming service or Netlix as opposed to traditional TV channels, using at most 20% of the remote's buttons.
  • Most respondents watch TV multiple times a week using this remote.
  • Despite regular use of the remote, most do not know what all the buttons do. Commonly used buttons are Power, Volume (up or down), source, arrows or OK.
  • Many users like to switch off the lights and use the TV and remote.
  • Surprisingly, even regular users needed to look at the remote in the light to know what all the buttons did.
